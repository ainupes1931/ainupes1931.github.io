KAPPA ALPHA PSI

Founded Jan 5, 1911 in Bloomington, Indiana

ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. was founded on January 5, 1911, at Indiana University Bloomington by ten visionary young men. The fraternity was established during a time of significant racial segregation and limited opportunities for African American students in higher education.

FOUNDERS

  • Elder Watson Diggs
  • Byron Kenneth Armstrong
  • Ezra Dee Alexander
  • Henry Tourner Asher
  • Marcus Peter Blakemore
  • Paul Waymond Caine
  • George Wesley Edmonds
  • Guy Levis Grant
  • Edward Giles Irvin
  • John Milton Lee

The fraternity was founded with the mission of promoting brotherhood, academic excellence, and community service. Its principles are embodied in the motto "Achievement in Every Field of Human Endeavor" and the cardinal principles of Achievement, Brotherhood, and Service.
